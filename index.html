Property Description

The subject is a 1.78-acre parcel located at 110 S Racepath St in Fayetteville, NC (Parcel ID: 0447-02-9017), zoned HI (Heavy Industrial). The site contains an existing building of approximately 1,800 SF, which is currently occupied on a partial, short-term basis by a small auto body shop tenant. The remainder of the parcel is open, unimproved land.

Given the minimal improvements relative to total site size, this analysis treats the parcel primarily as a land transaction, with the existing structure considered of incidental contributory influence on overall pricing. The 1.78-acre HI-zoned site is well-positioned for industrial or contractor uses common in this submarket.

Building size and parcel data per id.land and CoStar sources. All sizes approximate; verify prior to contract.

Submarket Notes

The Fayetteville HI submarket includes properties along Racepath St, Eastern Blvd, Jenkins St, Murchison Rd, and Pine St corridors. HI-zoned land in this area has historically traded at a wide range of prices per acre depending on access, size, improvements, and intended use.

Recent arms-length HI sales indicate a relevant range of approximately $100,000–$210,000/acre for similarly sized parcels with modest or no improvements, with smaller, better-located or better-improved parcels trading at a premium above that range. The subject’s Racepath St location and 1.78-acre scale fall within the mid-range of the comparable set.

Scope, Assumptions & Limiting Conditions

This Broker Price Opinion is prepared by Grant-Murray Real Estate, LLC for Cumberland County (the stated client) and is intended solely to support internal decision-making. It is not an appraisal, and no appraisal-level due diligence has been performed, including title review, survey review, environmental assessment, structural inspection, or measurement verification.

The analysis is based on information provided by or on behalf of the client, public records, id.land, and CoStar data believed reliable. Material changes to tenancy, zoning, site condition, encumbrances, or market conditions may impact the conclusions. The existence, terms, and enforceability of the existing auto body shop tenancy have not been independently verified and should be confirmed prior to any disposition action.

Disposition Notes

If Cumberland County proceeds with a disposition of this parcel, marketing to industrial and contractor-user buyers active in the local submarket would be recommended. Given the HI zoning, likely buyer profiles include small contractors, auto-related users, storage/logistics operators, and investors seeking HI-zoned land in the Fayetteville market.

Specific listing terms, cooperating broker compensation, and marketing strategy to be determined in coordination with the County.

Application to Subject

408 S Racepath St is the strongest comparable: same street, HI zoning, arms-length, 2024 sale. At 0.75 acres and $300,000/acre it is smaller and likely commands a size premium the subject cannot fully match; a downward adjustment is appropriate. 327 E Jenkins St (2.0 ac, HI, $104,500/acre) and 408 Pine St (2.97 ac, HI, $67,340/acre) bracket the subject from below given their larger sizes and lower $/acre. The 3100 Murchison Rd sale ($299,800/acre) is noted as a condemnation/DOT acquisition and is excluded from the central opinion range as potentially non-market. The 908 North St sale ($389,091/acre, LI zoning, 1.1 ac) provides an upper-bound reference but warrants a downward adjustment for superior access/exposure and LI vs. HI context.

Weighing these indicators, a price opinion in the range of $350,000–$425,000 (±$196,629–$238,764/acre) is supportable, with a recommended list price of $390,000 (~$219,101/acre).
